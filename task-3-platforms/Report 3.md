I used Google Forms to create and manage a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz for my batchmates. Google Forms allowed me to design a quiz with different types of questions, including multiple choice and short answer, making it interactive and easy to attempt. The responses were automatically collected and organized in Google Sheets, which helped in viewing and analyzing the data efficiently.

Through this task, I practised creating structured online forms, sharing them through a link, and integrating them into my GitHub README. I also learned how responses can be tracked in real time and how data can be interpreted using spreadsheets. Additionally, adding screenshots of both the form and the response sheet improved my documentation skills.

These tools will be very helpful academically as they make it easier to conduct surveys, collect feedback, and organize information for projects or research. Google Forms simplifies data collection, while Google Sheets helps in analysis and presentation. Overall, this activity enhanced my digital literacy and showed me how online tools can be used effectively for learning and collaboration.
